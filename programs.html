What we do, every day

Four pillars. Eighty villages. One unfinished promise.

Our programs are designed by the village, for the village. Each one is small enough to feel personal and old enough to have proved itself in the field for at least a decade.

Pillar 01

Shiksha — Education

Rural Knowledge Centers, scholarships, libraries and computer labs serving 2,400+ first-generation learners every year.

Pillar 02

Swasthya — Health

Mobile camps, maternal care, sanitation drives.

Pillar 03

Naari Shakti — Women & Youth

32 self-help groups, vocational training, scholarships for girls.

Pillar 04

Gaon Vikas — Rural Development

Water, sanitation, disaster relief and the steady infrastructure of village life — the slow work that doesn't make headlines but keeps a community whole.

Mahavir Vidya Mandir · Bishanpur
Pillar 01

Shiksha.
Education for everyone, at every age.

We began with a single two-room library in Bishanpur in 1994. Today, eight Rural Knowledge Centers stay open six days a week — with books in Hindi, Maithili and English, free Wi-Fi, and a librarian ready to read aloud to anyone who can't yet.

Rural Knowledge Centers8 community libraries open six days a week.
02
Computer literacy400 rural girls & boys trained in basic IT each year.
03
Scholarships140 children supported from class 6 through college.
04
Adult literacyEvening classes for parents and grandparents.

Free medical camp · Bishanpur
Pillar 02

Swasthya.
Care that comes to your door.

In villages where the nearest district hospital is two bus changes away, we bring the doctor to the people. A monthly mobile camp rotates through 18 villages — free check-ups, free medicine, and a referral system to Patna for anything we can't handle on site.

01
Mobile health campsMonthly rotation across 18 villages.
02
Eye-care drivesQuarterly screenings and free cataract surgeries.
03
Maternal healthAntenatal check-ups and trained midwife support.
04
Sanitation & WASHToilet construction and hygiene education in schools.

Girls' education · Madhubani
Pillar 03

Naari Shakti.
When she earns, the village changes.

Thirty-two self-help groups — pickle-makers, weavers, papad-rollers, tailors — federated to bargain with banks and bulk-buyers on equal terms. Last year, the federation rotated ₹18 lakh in micro-loans internally without a single default.

01
Self-help groups32 active SHGs across 24 villages.
02
Vocational trainingTailoring, beautician, Madhubani painting and food processing.
03
Girls' scholarshipsEarmarked stipends to keep girls in school past class 10.
04
Youth leadershipAnnual residential camp for class-11 students.

Pillar 04

Gaon Vikas.
The boring, beautiful work.

Hand pumps that work. Drains that drain. A relief kitchen during floods. None of it is glamorous — and exactly that is the point. We do the unsexy infrastructure work that lets every other program land softly.

02
Disaster responseFlood relief, ration kits and oxygen during COVID.
03
Tree plantation2,000+ saplings planted with school children every Van Mahotsav.
04
Panchayat advocacyHelping villages claim entitlements they're already owed.
