Our story

A trust born from a freedom fighter's last wish — and a village's refusal to forget him.

Mahavir Singh Memorial Trust was registered on 28 November 1992, in the village of Bishanpur, Madhubani. We are run almost entirely by neighbours, family members and fellow workers of the man we are named for.

Shri Mahavir Singh · freedom fighter
The founder · 1907 – 1992

Shri Mahavir Singh.

Born in 1907 in a small farming family of north Bihar, Shri Mahavir Singh joined the freedom struggle in his twenties — taking part in the Non-Cooperation and Quit India movements, hosting underground meetings, and twice serving time in colonial jails.

After independence, he refused a government post in Patna and returned to Bishanpur. He spent the next four decades as a grassroots social worker and political organiser — convening panchayat sabhas, leading land-reform campaigns, and standing with the poorest farmers of Madhubani when no one else would. When he died in early 1992, his comrades said the only memorial he would have wanted was another village uplifted.

"He never owned a wristwatch. He said the village would tell him what time it was."

— A fellow Sarvodaya worker, 1993
What we stand for

Mission, vision, and the values that hold us steady.

◇

Mission

To eradicate illiteracy and bring health, dignity and opportunity to the rural masses of north Bihar — without political or sectarian agenda.

◈

Vision

A Madhubani where every child finishes school, every mother delivers safely, and every grandparent can read a letter from a grandchild without help.

◉

Values

Seva (service), Saadgi (simplicity), Sachaai (truth) and Saamuhik Shakti (collective strength) — the four words painted above our office door since 1993.

Three decades of work

A timeline.

Small, steady milestones — most of them measured in classrooms opened or wells dug, not in column inches.

28 Nov 1992

Trust founded

Registered in Madhubani on the first anniversary of Shri Mahavir Singh's passing.

1994

First Rural Knowledge Center

A two-room community library opens in Bishanpur — the trust's first permanent building.

2001

Free eye-care camps begin

In partnership with visiting ophthalmologists from Patna and Darbhanga.

2008

First computer lab

Twelve desktop machines and a borrowed generator — our first venture into digital literacy.

2015

Women's SHG federation

Self-help groups across 24 villages federate to access bank credit on better terms.

2020

COVID relief network

Door-to-door ration delivery and oxygen-concentrator loaning during the second wave.

2026

Eight RKCs, 80+ villages

Today: eight Rural Knowledge Centers, a mobile health van, and over 12,000 lives touched each year.
