Milestone 22 March 2025

GGLASC inaugurated at the Trust's Bishnupur campus

In response to PM Modi's call to compile a database of Girmitiya descendants, the Trust inaugurated the Global Girmitiya Lineage Archive & Searching Centre (GGLASC) — dedicated to preserving and sharing the history of the Indian indentured diaspora.

Read more →

The Global Girmitiya Lineage Archive & Searching Centre (GGLASC), established at the Mahavir Singh Memorial Trust in Bishanpur village, is now operational. The centre is dedicated to preserving, researching and sharing the history of the Girmitiya diaspora — the millions of Indians who were taken to colonial plantations as indentured labourers in the 19th and early 20th centuries.

The Centre helps families across the world trace their ancestral roots and restore lost identities and dignity.

For coverage of this story, see our Press page.

[ Optional photo from the visit ]
Event 13 December 2025

Surinamese scholar Chan Choenni visits the memorial wall

Scholar Chan Choenni, a Girmitiya descendant born in Suriname and based in the Netherlands, visited the memorial plaque at the Trust honouring his ancestor — as part of the ongoing Girmitiyalogy Lectures and Pilgrimage to Bharat.

Read more →

Accompanied by his wife Binarai Makhan, Chan Choenni paid tribute to Thakur Kishundayal Singh Harnandan Singh — a Girmitiya from Chhapra (now Saran district) who was indentured to colonial plantations more than a century ago.

The plaque was inaugurated on 22 March 2025 by Shardhanand Harinandan Singh. The Trust's memorial wall honours India's freedom fighters alongside the largely overlooked indentured labourers who built the diaspora.

Coverage of this visit appeared in The Times of India — see our Press page.

Mahavir Singh Memorial Trustमहावीर सिंह स्मारक न्यास Est. 1992स्था. 1992

A public charitable trust founded by Dr. Binod Kumar Singh in 1992, in memory of his father, the freedom fighter Mahavir Singh — serving rural Bihar through education, health and dignity ever since.डॉ. विनोद कुमार सिंह द्वारा 1992 में अपने पिता, स्वतंत्रता सेनानी महावीर सिंह की स्मृति में स्थापित एक सार्वजनिक चैरिटेबल ट्रस्ट — तब से ग्रामीण बिहार में शिक्षा, स्वास्थ्य और सम्मान के लिए कार्यरत।
